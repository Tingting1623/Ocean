THE DEEP SEA
The deep sea is the lowest layer in the ocean, at a depth of 1000 fathoms or more. Little or no light penetrates this part of the ocean.

LIGHT
Natural light does not penetrate the deep ocean, with the exception of the upper parts of the mesopelagic. Since photosynthesis is not possible, plants and phytoplankton cannot live in this zone.
PRESSURE
Because pressure in the ocean increases by about 1 atmosphere for every 10 meters of depth, the amount of pressure experienced by many marine organisms is extreme.
SALINITY
Salinity is remarkably constant throughout the deep sea, at about 35 parts per thousand.[4] There are some minor differences in salinity, but none that are ecologically significant, except in the Mediterranean and Red Seas.
